# Product Brief: SchoolCronicle

## Executive Summary

SchoolCronicle is a web app for schools to track and communicate day-to-day student progress in one place. It gives teachers a fast way to log classroom events, assignments, and observations, while giving students and parents a clear, reliable timeline of what matters.

Many schools still rely on fragmented tools (paper notes, chat groups, spreadsheets, and email). That creates missed context, delayed communication, and extra admin work. SchoolCronicle aims to replace this with a simple, role-based experience built for daily use.

## The Problem

School communication and student tracking are often spread across too many channels.

- Teachers lose time duplicating updates across systems.
- Parents do not always get timely, structured visibility.
- Students miss expectations when assignments and feedback are scattered.
- School leadership lacks consistent data on engagement and follow-through.

## The Solution

SchoolCronicle provides a shared activity timeline for each class and student, with focused workflows for teachers, students, and parents.

- Teachers can create updates, assignments, and notes quickly.
- Students can see what is due, what changed, and what needs action.
- Parents can follow verified updates without monitoring multiple channels.
- Admins can monitor adoption and communication health across classes.

## What Makes This Different

- Built around school communication cadence (daily updates, weekly summaries, term progress).
- Prioritizes clarity over feature sprawl, with role-specific views.
- Designed for trust: structured records instead of disappearing chat messages.
- Practical advantage is execution speed and usability for non-technical users.

## Who This Serves

- **Primary users:** Teachers who need to document and share classroom progress efficiently.
- **Secondary users:** Students and parents who need a clear view of expectations and progress.
- **Tertiary users:** School administrators who need visibility into consistency and outcomes.

## Success Criteria

- 80%+ of teachers in pilot classes post at least 3 updates per week.
- 60%+ of parents in pilot classes view updates weekly.
- 30% reduction in teacher-reported communication overhead within one term.
- 25% reduction in "missed assignment due to communication gap" incidents in pilot groups.

## Scope

### In Scope (V1)

- Role-based authentication (teacher, student, parent, admin).
- Class and student timelines with timestamped entries.
- Assignment posting with due dates and status.
- Basic notification delivery (in-app + email digest).
- Search/filter for recent updates by class or student.

### Out of Scope (V1)

- Full grading and report-card replacement.
- Native mobile apps (web-first responsive experience only).
- Advanced analytics dashboards beyond core usage metrics.
- Third-party SIS/LMS deep integrations.

## Vision

If SchoolCronicle succeeds, it becomes the school's trusted operational timeline for learning and communication, not just a messaging layer. Over the next 2-3 years, it can evolve into a system that helps schools identify support needs earlier, strengthen parent engagement, and reduce administrative friction through consistent, high-quality classroom data.

## Assumptions To Validate

- The initial target segment is K-12 schools (not higher education).
- Communication and assignment visibility are the highest-priority pain points.
- A responsive web app is sufficient for early adoption before native mobile.
- Schools can pilot without deep SIS/LMS integration in phase one.
